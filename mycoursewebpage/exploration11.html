The regions in AWS are basically geographical areas consisting of multiple, isolated locations known as Availability Zones or AZs(typically 3). Each AZ can be multiple data centers, and at full scale can be hundreds of thousands of servers. AZs are physically separated by meaningful distance from each other but are interconnected with high bandwidth, low-latency networking.
